FAQ

Honest answers
to common questions.

What does a typical project cost?

Most projects land between $3K–$25K depending on scope. A landing page starts around $3K, full brand + website packages are typically $10K–$20K, and product UI work is scoped per sprint. You'll always get a fixed quote after the discovery call — no hourly surprises.

How long does a project take?

A focused landing page usually ships in 2–3 weeks. A full website + brand identity runs 4–6 weeks. Product sprints are typically 2-week cycles. I'll always give you a realistic timeline up-front, not a wishful one.

Do you work with agencies or in-house teams?

Both. I often white-label for studios needing extra senior firepower, and I plug into in-house product teams as a fractional designer/developer. Either way, you get one point of contact who's accountable for the outcome.

What's your availability right now?

I take on 3–5 projects per quarter to keep quality high. Currently booking into Q3 2026 with 2 spots open. If your timeline is flexible, jump on a call and we'll figure out a start date that works.

Do you handle content, copy, and SEO too?

I can structure and write core copy as part of a build, and every project ships with on-page SEO fundamentals (semantic HTML, schema, performance, meta). For deep content strategy or technical SEO, I bring in trusted specialists I've worked with for years.

What happens after launch?

Every project includes 30 days of post-launch support for tweaks, bug fixes, and questions. After that, you can book ongoing retainer hours for updates, experiments, or new features. You also keep all source files, repos, and a Notion playbook — no lock-in, ever.
